# Collaboration is hard

Collaboration is hard because every collaborator doesn't have the information that's in all the other collaborators' heads. Right? 

When you collaborate with someone, you don't have all the same information as them. You have different memories.. and senses.. and thoughts.. You can't read their mind. And the same is true in reverse. They don't have your information. This makes collaboration hard. 

Coordination is hard. Sometimes, to work together, you need to make sure you make room for each other or spread out or bunch up or whatever to succeed in what you're doing. If there are two jobs and two collaborators, it often makes sense for each collaborator to do one job each. You need to decide who does which one. Or alternatively, if each job requires three hands, then you need both collaborators to do each job together one after the other, so you need to agree on which one to do first. This is coordination. Sometimes it doesn't matter *what* you do: You still need to *coordinate* it. 

Strategy is hard. Sometimes— Always: There is always an option: There are always options. In every moment we face choices. What do we do? What's best to do? Even refusing to make a choice is a kind of choice. Without [perfect information](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perfect_information), you can't know what the right choice is. And in the real world, unfortunately, no one has perfect information. So you can only guess based on what limited information you have. And as we discussed:

Every collaborator has different information. Everyone knows things that you do not. You know things that no else does.
