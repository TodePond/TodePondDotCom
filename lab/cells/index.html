Rect

Another way of showing cells is on a canvas.

First make a canvas.

<canvas></canvas>

Don't forget, we only want a 100 by 100 grid.

<canvas width="100" height="100">

Don't listen to anyone who tells you your canvas needs to be the same size as your screen. This is a common misconception I see. If we want a 100 by 100 grid, we only need a 100 by 100 canvas.

To draw in the canvas, you need its context. Get the context.

<script>
const canvas = document.querySelector("canvas")
const context = canvas.getContext("2d")
</script>

If you think about it, a cell is just a tiny rectangle. Luckily, our context knows how to draw rectangles. 

context.fillRect(10, 20, 1, 1)

In the above code, we draw a rectangle in column 10, in row 20.

The 1 and 1 refer to how many cells to draw at that location. In this case, I've decided to draw 1 cell only. But if you want to, you could draw more in one go.
